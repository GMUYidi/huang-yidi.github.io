## Overview

This late-breaking poster presented ongoing work on **Reduced-Order Dynamics and Nonlinear Control of Listing-Constrained Eye Movements**.

The work studies how human-like eye movements can be represented with reduced-order dynamics while respecting Listing's law and half-angle behavior. The poster connects nonlinear state feedback control, parameter identification from human saccades, and experimental validation on a robotic binocular eye platform.

The poster highlighted:

- A reduced-order formulation for 3D eye rotation dynamics
- Nonlinear feedback control for yaw and pitch movement
- Listing-constrained torsion behavior through the control design
- Parameter identification from experimentally recorded human saccades
- Experimental validation of human-like foveation trajectories

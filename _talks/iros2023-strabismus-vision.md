## Overview

In this talk, I presented our work **See What a Strabismus Patient Sees Using Eye Robots**, which introduces a robotic binocular eye platform for simulating and visualizing what patients with strabismus may see.

The work combines a cable-driven robotic eye system, human eye-movement data, onboard cameras, and a homography-based binocular vision fusion pipeline. The goal is to make otherwise subjective visual symptoms, such as double or blurry vision, easier to observe, communicate, and analyze.

The presentation highlighted:

- The robotic binocular eye platform and its camera-based visual scene capture
- A data-driven pipeline for replaying human eye angle data on robotic eyes
- Homography-based fusion of the left-eye and right-eye views
- Experimental validation using healthy-subject data and strabismus-patient misalignment data
